Best exchange for daytrading

One of the most popular cryptocurrency trading platforms is Bitfinex. Even though the Bitfinex exchange was hacked in 2016 it remained hugely popular: the trading interface is great, margin trading is available for most cryptocurrencies and there is the p2p lending option that can be profitable when you don't want to trade.

However in December 2017 Bitfinex stopped accepting new users because (as most crypto exchanges) it got overloaded and couldn't technically keep up with the influx of people.

Your best options for 2018 are the following trading platforms:

  • Binance - lists new coins regularly, has two different trading views (basic and advanced), is quite reliable doesn't require berification although it limits withdrawals without.
  • Mercatox - One of the still lesser known altcoin exchanges which means it is less overloaded with users. Mercatox is also quick to list new cryptocurrencies.
  • GDAX - You will need to verify but GDAX is the most popular trading platform in the US so you will get good deep markets.
  • BitMEX - A derivative platform for more advanced traders.

Cryptocurrency arbitrage
When you start looking into bitcoin exchange to buy or sell on, you will notice that prices differ from one another. One typical example is GDAX vs Bitfinex, Australian exchanges vs Europen exchanges, or Japanese or Korean markets vs the western cryptocurrency markets. If you can find a way to move the fiat currency you can make money on this difference in pricing. To take advantage of these arbitrage opportunities you can make use of virtual bank accounts that are available with apps such as N26 or Revolut. They are currently open only for residents within the European Economic Area (EEA) and Switzerland but Revolut Business is also open to companies registered in EEA.
